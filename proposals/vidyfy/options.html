Draft Monthly Retainer Options • Vidyfy Phase One Creator Growth
Current Objective, Not Future Vision

Get the first good creators in and help them succeed.

Phase one is not broad brand marketing. It is creator education, targeted outreach, white-glove onboarding, and a proof loop that turns early wins into credible momentum. These options keep the work centered on Instagram, TikTok, and direct creator recruitment.

What this proposal assumes

Vidyfy grows now by becoming useful to early creators before it has network effects. The work needs to be disciplined, practical, and easy to repeat.

  • Primary channels: Instagram, TikTok, and direct outreach
  • LinkedIn stays light and founder-led, or gets skipped
  • Goal is creator acquisition plus activation, not vanity reach
  • Automation supports the team internally so the process does not become manual chaos

Phase One Logic

The growth loop is simple on purpose: useful creator-facing content earns attention, direct outreach turns that attention into conversations, white-glove onboarding increases the chance of first posts, and those first wins become the proof that makes the next wave easier.

01

Creator education first

Use universal creator tips, filming examples, hook breakdowns, and workflow content to attract the right kind of early creator before asking them to join anything.

02

Recruit with personal outreach

Growth comes from direct DMs, curated invite lists, and early collaboration pitches, not from waiting for a cold audience to discover the platform on its own.

03

Activate, then prove

The job is not done at signup. The real win is getting creators to post, stay active, and generate enough proof that the next creators have a reason to say yes.

Shared assumptions across all options

  • Monthly retainer structure instead of hourly ambiguity
  • Existing editor and designer can be used when available
  • No paid media buying, ad spend, or creator incentive budget included
  • Focus stays on current creator-platform growth, not future B2B roadmap positioning
  • Automation work supports internal ops, tracking, prompts, and follow-up systems
  • Deliverables emphasize consistency and learning, not inflated volume for its own sake

Three Ways To Structure The Work

The cleanest way to price this is by stage, not by visible hourly math. The first option is a setup month. The second is the core ongoing engine. The third adds the hybrid layer, stronger proof output, and more internal systems support.

What Success Looks Like

These are not hard promises. They are the signals the work should be improving if the phase-one strategy is actually working.

01

Qualified creators contacted

Curated creators who actually fit the platform, not inflated lead lists.

02

Reply rate

Whether the invite angle, proof, and creator pitch are strong enough to start real conversations.

03

Onboarding conversations

Signals that early creators see enough value to move beyond passive curiosity.

04

First-post rate

The clearest sign that signup friction is low enough and the onboarding support is working.

05

Repeat-post proof

If creators come back and post again, the platform starts to become usable proof instead of a pitch deck idea.

Recommendation

The cleanest structure is usually staged. Use the first month to get the system right, then move into the ongoing engine. Only step into the hybrid layer once the base motion is actually working.

Recommended path: start with Strategy + Setup at $3,000 for month one, then move into Content + Outreach at $4,000/month. Move to Hybrid Growth at $5,000/month only if Vidyfy wants the extra trust layer, proof content, and internal systems support.

Decision shortcut: if they are still debating what to say, choose setup. If they know what to say but need creator conversations, choose content plus outreach. If creators are entering but not enough trust or proof exists yet, choose hybrid growth.

Pricing note: This version is more honest for the stage. The lower pricing works only if the deliverables stay tight, the process gets leveraged through templates and automation, and the scope does not quietly turn into a full marketing department.

Scope note: Creator incentive budget, paid media, platform ad spend, and heavy daily community moderation are not included in these numbers.
