## RESTAURANTS / FOOD

### Who's Crushing It
| Account | Followers | What Makes It Work |
|---------|-----------|-------------------|
| @frankprisinzano | 500K+ | Raw phone-filmed kitchen chaos. No recipes, no measurements, just vibes. Personality beats production. |
| @eggslut | 257K | Cult food truck to brand. Close-up cheese pulls, dripping egg shots. The food IS the content. |
| @fatherandfish | 58K | One hero product (fish burger) + funny skits. Proof you don't need a big menu. |
| @badroman.nyc | 45K | Zero to 45K in months. Moody, cinematic food + irreverent brand voice. |
| @tartinebakery | 487K | Natural-light bread photography that looks like a cookbook. Warm, simple, consistent. |

### The Pattern
- **Messy > polished.** The best restaurant accounts show real food being made, not plated studio shots.
- **One hero item wins.** You don't need to show the whole menu. Pick the thing that makes people stop scrolling.
- **Personality drives follows.** @frankprisinzano has 500K followers because of HIM, not just his food.
- **Process content outperforms finished plates.** Show the sear, the flip, the pour, the melt.
- **Urgency hooks work.** "Selling out by 7pm" > "Come try our new burger."

### What to Steal
1. Film the cook making the signature dish (phone, no editing)
2. Extreme close-up of the best visual moment (cheese pull, sizzle, pour)
3. "This is what [time] looks like here" — show the energy
4. Sell the sellout: "This runs out every [day]. Get here early."
5. Show the line, the crowd, the full dining room — social proof

### Formats That Work
- Reels: kitchen action, food assembly, BTS (80% of growth)
- Static: hero product shots with warm natural light (anchor feed aesthetic)
- Carousels: menu spotlights, "5 things to order" guides

### The Pattern
- **Educational hooks build authority.** "Most clients wait too long before starting Botox" > "Botox available, book now."
- **Before/after is king.** It's the universal engagement format for aesthetics.
- **Approachable beats clinical.** The best accounts feel like a friend who happens to be an expert.
- **Staff faces drive trust.** People want to see who's touching their face.
- **Satisfying procedure videos get saves.** Filler injections, laser passes, peel reveals.

### What to Steal
1. "The question I get asked most about [treatment]" — educational Reel
2. Before/after carousel with specific details ("6 weeks post-treatment, 2 sessions")
3. "What [treatment] actually looks like" — real procedure, no filter
4. Staff member answering a DM question on camera
5. Client reaction at reveal moment (genuine, not staged)

### Formats That Work
- Reels: procedure videos, before/after reveals, Q&A with practitioners (80%)
- Carousels: before/after comparisons, treatment breakdowns, skincare routines
- Static: clean product shots, staff headshots, space photography

### The Pattern

- **Transformation stories drive engagement.** "6 months ago I couldn't do a pull-up."
- **Class energy is contagious.** Film the room mid-workout — music, movement, sweat.
- **Trainer personalities build loyalty.** Feature instructors as characters, not just staff.

### What to Steal
1. "If you've been saying 'I'll start next week'..." — motivational hook
2. Film 15 seconds of a class at peak energy (music, movement, community)
3. Member spotlight: "Why [name] keeps coming back"
4. "What your first day actually looks like" — remove the intimidation
5. Trainer teaching one move with personality

### Formats That Work
- Reels: class energy clips, trainer tips, member stories (90% of growth)
- Static: moody studio photography, community group shots

## UNIVERSAL PATTERNS (Across All Industries)

1. **Reels drive growth. Static photography anchors the feed.** Use both.
2. **Community/member/client content outperforms polished brand content.** Show real people.
3. **The best accounts have a visual identity you'd recognize without the handle.** Consistency wins.
4. **Local expertise content outperforms product-only posts.** Be the expert for your area.
5. **Before/after and transformation content is the universal engagement driver.**
6. **Personality beats production value.** A phone-filmed authentic moment > a studio shoot with no soul.
7. **One hero product/service is enough.** You don't need to show everything you do.
8. **Process content (making, cooking, treating, cutting) gets saves.** People love watching work happen.
