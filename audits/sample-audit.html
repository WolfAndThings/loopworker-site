The pattern: Your top posts create urgency or curiosity. Your worst posts are announcements with no emotional pull. The food is the same — the difference is the caption.

What I'd Fix First

1. Your Hooks

Your captions start with the thing instead of the feeling. Nobody stops scrolling for "New taco on the menu." They stop for "This sold out in 2 hours yesterday."

Before

"Come try our new birria taco! Available now."

After

"We made 40 birria tacos this morning. They were gone before lunch. Making 60 tomorrow."

Before

"Happy Taco Tuesday!"

After

"The thing nobody orders but should: our al pastor with pineapple slaw. Trust me."

2. Your Content Mix

  • 80% of your posts are menu/product photos — too repetitive
  • No behind-the-scenes content (your kitchen IS the content)
  • No customer moments or social proof
  • No urgency or "selling out" content
  • 40% food close-ups with urgency hooks
  • 25% behind-the-scenes (prep, griddle, kitchen energy)
  • 20% customer/community moments (the line, the crowd, reactions)
  • 15% personality (your story, why you do this, the team)

3. Your CTAs

You don't tell people what to do. Every post should end with a reason to come in, DM, or save.

  • "Come in before it's gone."
  • "DM us for catering."
  • "Tag someone who needs this."
  • "Save this for your next visit."

What Changes Visually

  • Lighting direction — direct flash or warm tungsten instead of flat phone flash
  • Composition — intentional framing, not centered snapshots
  • Color consistency — every image lives in the same brand palette
  • Texture and detail — close-ups that show the sear, the melt, the grain
  • Human moments — people in frame, not just empty plates
  • Campaign thinking — images work as a set, not random singles
