Where You're Losing Customers

Content that converts comes down to three things. Here's where you stand on each.

Visual Quality

Needs Work

Your food looks good in person, but your photos don't do it justice. Flat lighting, phone flash, no depth. When someone scrolls past your post, it looks like every other restaurant — not like a place they need to visit.

  • Flat overhead lighting with no direction or shadow
  • Phone flash washes out textures — the sear, the melt, the char
  • No close-ups that make people feel the food
  • Background clutter competing with the subject

What we'd fix

  • Direct flash or warm tungsten — creates texture and depth
  • Tight crops that show the cheese pull, the sear marks, the steam
  • Clean backgrounds — the food is the only subject
  • Every image looks intentional, not accidental

Feed Consistency

Needs Work

Your feed has no visual identity. Someone landing on your profile can't tell in 3 seconds what your brand feels like. Each post looks like it was shot on a different day, in a different style, with no connection to the next.

  • No consistent color palette across posts
  • Random mix of dark, bright, filtered, unfiltered
  • No recognizable "look" — nothing that says "this is us"
  • Grid looks scattered, not curated

What we'd fix

  • Lock a brand palette — warm amber + chocolate brown + cream
  • Same lighting style across every post — direct flash, warm tones
  • Campaign sets — 6 images that feel like they belong together
  • Your grid becomes a brand, not a photo dump

Engagement & Conversion

Below Average

You're getting views but not action. Your engagement rate is 1.2% — the industry benchmark for restaurants is 3-5%. People see your food but they're not saving, sharing, commenting, or walking in the door.

  • Captions are informational, not emotional — "New burger available!"
  • No hooks that stop the scroll — first line is generic
  • No urgency — nothing that says "come tonight"
  • No CTAs — you never tell people what to do next

What we'd fix

  • Every caption starts with a hook — "This sold out by 7pm last night"
  • Urgency built into every post — scarcity, timing, FOMO
  • Clear CTA on every post — "Come in before it's gone" / "Tag someone"
  • Content designed for saves and shares, not just likes
