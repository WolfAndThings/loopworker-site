◆ Case Study · Bay Area Bubble Events Co. · The Sprint

Kind Bubbles · $754 → mapped a $54–65K Y1 path.

Chris Steele runs Kind Bubbles — a Bay Area kids' bubble-events company. Biochemist turned teacher turned single dad turned founder. He paid $754 for the Sprint Lite tier on May 1, 2026. We delivered eight docs, scraped three live competitors, and modeled three Y1 revenue scenarios. Real client. Real numbers. Public.

BrandKind Bubbles · @kind.bubbles
FounderChris Steele
Sprint$700 · 8 docs · 5 days
Output4 pillars · 30 hooks · 30-day calendar

The brief

Chris is a biochemist by training. Spent years in classrooms before becoming a single dad to a kid who lit up around bubbles. The events business grew out of that — kids' parties, school sensory days, the occasional corporate gig. Bay Area, founder-led, no team, no investor, posting consistently to @kind.bubbles but not converting follows into bookings.

He found me through an Upwork conversation after his old IG account got banned and he was rebuilding from scratch. The brief was simple: tell me what's working, what's not, who I'm competing with, and what to make next month. He didn't want a deck. He wanted instructions.

The shock finding

We pulled 55 posts from the live feed. Sorted by saves and shares, the pattern was immediate: founder-on-camera content converted 3–4× event footage. Chris was sitting on his most valuable asset — himself, talking, on his iPhone — and was instead leading the feed with bubble close-ups and event b-roll.

Not because the bubble shots were bad. Because the founder shots had the trust signal that turns a parent from "interesting" to "I'm DM'ing him to book my kid's birthday." That trust signal was being buried by under-using the most under-used asset on the feed.

The four content pillars

Pulled directly from what was already working in the scrape data — not from a creator-economy template. Four pillars, each tagged to a sales job, each with a target ratio and example references.

◆ Founder & FamilyThe biochemist-dad-with-bubbles voice. Highest-converting pillar in the scrape. Trust + relatability + authority in one cut. Founder Story always gets the sharpest treatment.
◆ Reaction & ShowReal kids reacting to bubbles in real time. Save-magnet content. Parents send these to other parents.
◆ Bubble ScienceWhy bubbles work as a sensory and developmental tool. The "calm-down clip" pillar. Biochemist credentials get used here.
◆ Counter-PositionWhat Kind Bubbles is not. The pillar that pulls Chris out of head-to-head competition with party clowns and into the wellness-coded sensory-experience studio lane.

The live-data reveal — three live competitors

We pulled live data via our live-data pipeline on the three biggest Bay Area bubble entertainers. Pricing pages, IG feeds, booking copy, recent post performance. Real receipts, not vibes:

  • SF Bubble Man — heavy SF wedding + corporate positioning, premium pricing, almost no founder-voice content. Lane is "spectacle."
  • The Bubble Lady (Marin) — softer, more parent-facing, mid-tier pricing, leans into parties. Lane is "performer."
  • Miss Molly Bubbles (San Jose) — strongest IG presence of the three, but generic kids-entertainment voice. Lane is "entertainer."

None of them were occupying the biochemist-dad-with-a-thesis-on-sensory-regulation lane. That positioning shift — out of "bubble entertainer" and into "wellness-coded sensory-experience studio" — was the unlock. Same product, different shelf, way better margin.

The 30-day sprint plan

Eight docs delivered, five days from kickoff. Doc 8 was the sprint calendar — shoot-ready, beat-by-beat:

  • 8 reels with full beat-by-beat shoot lists, voiceover scripts, paste-ready captions
  • 4 educational carousels built around the bubble-science pillar
  • 1 pinned founder anchor — the Chris-on-camera intro reel that becomes the brand's home base
  • 16 daily Story prompts for the in-between days
  • 30-day calendar with pillar mix locked: 40% Founder & Family, 25% Reaction, 20% Science, 15% Counter-Position
  • Production tips — gear, what to avoid, what to repeat, how to shoot at events without the kids being weird about the camera

The ROI projection · three scenarios

Conservative~$19–23K Y1. Existing booking velocity continues, founder pillar lifts inquiry rate ~30%, no operator network, no corporate gigs.
Realistic~$54–65K Y1. Pillar mix shipped consistently. Founder content compounds. School sensory-day gigs come online. One small corporate.
Aggressive~$148–193K Y1. Therapy Dad authority track activated, B2B corporate-wellness pipeline engaged, first operator licensed in a second city.

The audit didn't promise the aggressive number. It mapped what it would take to get there — and made the realistic number look like the obvious floor if Chris ships the calendar.
