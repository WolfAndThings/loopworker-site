The three established Bay Area bubble entertainers — SF Bubble Man, The Bubble Lady, Miss Molly Bubbles — own different geographies and price tiers, but none of them own the "biochemist + emotional healing + science of wonder" position that Kind Bubbles already occupies. Publish a rate card. Rebecca's pricing transparency is a buyer-trust accelerator. Kind Bubbles' site doesn't show prices today. Add a tiered table: Private Booking · School Assembly · Corporate Pop-Up · Wellness Day. Mirror her structure.

Kind Bubbles' site is currently abstract ("mindful play"). Add 5–8 named tricks the kids will see — parents can picture them, kids will remember them, IG can show them.

Finally: South Bay + San Jose are uncovered by her. Pitch Palo Alto / Mountain View / San Jose schools first — Bubble Lady has explicitly given up that geography.

Threat 03 · South Bay / San Jose

The multi-disciplinary entertainer (bubbles + balloons + face paint)

Miss Molly is the only one of the three operating in the South Bay / San Jose area Rebecca abandoned. She's a generalist — not just bubbles.

Miss Molly Bubbles
missmollybubbles.com · San Jose / Silicon Valley · multi-act
Low threat
What she wins on

Service depth. Bubble shows + magic + balloons + face painting + stilt walking — buys her into more parties than a bubble-only entertainer would get. Multiple show formats: Outdoor Bubble Show, Indoor Bubble Magic, Hands-on Bubble Stations, Fancy Show, Bubble + Magic Combo.

Where she's weak

No specialization. Generalist energy reads as "kids party entertainer for hire" — not a brand. Site is bold but DIY. No pricing visible. Shop page says "COMING SOON!" for a year+ at this point. No social presence. No founder story. The brand is "Miss Molly does many things."

What Chris should steal

Don't go multi-act. Miss Molly's spread is the lesson — adding face paint and balloons would dilute the Kind Bubbles brand to a generic kids' party entertainer. Stay specialized. Bubble + science + healing is a category. Bubble + balloons is a search result on GigSalad.

DO steal: her menu of show formats. "Outdoor Bubble Show / Indoor Bubble Magic / Hands-on Stations / Fancy Show / Bubble + Magic Combo." Kind Bubbles' booking page doesn't currently break out formats. Add 3 named experiences: The Sensory Pop-Up (kids), The Wellness Reset (corporate), The Healing Circle (community/private).

Refined positioning · post-research

Kind Bubbles is not a kids' party brand.
It's a wellness-coded sensory-experience studio
that happens to specialize in bubbles.

Stop competing with bubble entertainers. Compete with corporate wellness facilitators — and beat them at warmth.

SF Bubble Man competes with bubble entertainers and wins on tenure. Bubble Lady competes with performers and wins on creativity. Miss Molly competes on breadth. Kind Bubbles, framed correctly, doesn't compete with any of them — it sits next to companies like Headspace, Calm, Modern Health, Insight Timer as a wellness-experience provider whose modality is play.

That frame justifies the higher price tier. It opens corporate-wellness budgets that bubble-entertainers can't access. It pulls schools toward SEL/social-emotional-learning conversations rather than "what entertainers do you have." And it gives Chris a category-of-one even within the Bay Area.

Next: rewrite the Kind Bubbles homepage hero with the wellness-experience-studio frame. Add a Corporate Wellness page distinct from the Private Booking page. Pitch the same ten companies SF Bubble Man already worked with — but the contact is the wellness or DEI lead, not the events coordinator.
