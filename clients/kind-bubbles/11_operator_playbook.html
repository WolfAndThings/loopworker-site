The job

Five operators.
One brand voice.
Zero quality drift.

The whole network depends on one thing: a kid in Atlanta gets the same experience a kid in San Francisco gets. Same warmth, same scientific framing, same parental connection moment. Operators are not entertainers-for-hire — they're licensed local representatives of the Kind Bubbles voice.

Mad Science scaled internationally because a parent in any city knows the show their kid will get is the same show every other city gets — same standards, same trained operator, same brand experience. Consistency is the moat. Operator recruitment is how the moat gets dug. (Specific Mad Science territory count not freshly scraped — treat as structural pattern, not benchmark.) 01
Application + Screening
Self-serve · landing page → 90-min phone call
Week 1–2
The funnel
Landing page at kindbubbles.com/become-a-facilitator with the founder's story up top, the model below, and a Typeform-style application embedded inline. Application asks the right questions; screening call confirms the warmth + voice fit.
Application questions
Open-ended, voice-revealing, low on credentials, high on character.
  1. Tell me about the last time you genuinely surprised a kid with something.
  2. What's a parenting moment from your own life that shaped how you'd run a Kind Bubbles event?
  3. If a kid is shy and won't come into the bubble circle, what do you do?
  4. Why this brand, and why this city?
  5. What other gigs / income would Kind Bubbles fit alongside? (We're a fit, not a takeover.)
  6. How would your closest friend describe how you are with kids?
Screening call (90 min)
Two halves. First 45 minutes: founder walks them through the brand story, the model, the ask. Second 45 minutes: applicant does a "tell me about a time" interview + one practical question ("show me how you'd introduce yourself to a 5-year-old"). Warmth, vulnerability, listening — not credentials.
02
Training Week in SF
5-day immersion · founder-led · in-home + on-site
Week 3–4
Why in-person
The voice transfers in person, not over Zoom. Operators stay in SF for the week, attend Chris's actual events, mix solution at the kitchen sink, learn the script through repetition with real kids. Industry-standard service franchises invest meaningful in-person training time per new partner. The 5-day training week is the right floor — enough to transfer the voice, the show structure, and the brand standards face-to-face.
5-day curriculum
Day 1
Brand + voice
Founder story session (Chris on his journey). The four pillars. The Therapy Dad voice. What we say, what we don't say, why.
Day 2
The science
Bubble chemistry. Solution mixing (ratios, water temp, glycerin sourcing). Operators leave able to teach the science to kids and parents.
Day 3
Show structure
The 60-min Kind Bubbles experience flow. Opening, science demos, hands-on play, parent moment, close. Same beats every time.
Day 4
Edge cases + parent handling
Shy kids, sensory-sensitive kids, overwhelmed parents. The 5 things to say + when to step back. Plus safety, allergies, weather pivots, refunds.
Day 5
Live event + sign-off
Operator co-runs an actual Bay Area booking with Chris. Founder signs off in person. Operator leaves training week with a certificate, a starter kit (wand set, solution recipe, branded materials), and a city launch plan.
03
Brand-Standards Agreement
Lawyer-drafted licensing structure · partnership + brand-use terms
Week 4 (signed before launch)
What it covers
Six things.
  1. Operator's license to use the Kind Bubbles name, mark, scripts, and content in their city.
  2. Partnership structure: bookings flow through corporate; specific terms set with a franchise lawyer.
  3. Quality standards: must run the standard show structure, must meet weekly check-ins for first 90 days, must use approved materials.
  4. Geographic exclusivity within their metro (defined territory) — protects operator from cannibalization.
  5. IP ownership: all photography, video, content created stays with Kind Bubbles brand (operator gets usage rights for personal portfolio).
  6. Termination + repurchase: 30-day notice each way; corporate has option to buy back the territory if operator exits.
Partnership rationale
The first 5 operators come in on a no-upfront-fee partnership — easier yes for operators who don't have a large lump sum to invest up-front. Once the brand has 5–10 operators and the model is proven, the structure can transition into a formal franchise system. Specific terms will be set with a franchise lawyer in Phase 2 — the recommendation in this doc is to keep the first wave as light as possible to prove the playbook.

Who fits · who doesn't

The screen filters for
warmth + voice + work ethic.

Most kids' entertainers are great at the show but not the brand. Most therapists are great at the brand but not the show. Kind Bubbles operators are the rare overlap — and that's why screening matters more than scaling fast.

✓ Must have
  • 2+ years working with kids 3–10 (any context — entertainer, teacher, OT, camp counselor)
  • Comfortable on camera (operator is also a content creator in their market)
  • Clean background check + active insurance
  • Reliable transportation + ability to travel within metro
  • Real lived warmth with kids — passes the screening "shy kid" question
  • Fits the Kind Bubbles voice — calm, curious, not performative
  • Available for 5-day training week in SF (corporate covers travel + lodging)
  • Can commit to ≥30 events / year minimum
× Disqualifiers
  • Any history of inappropriate conduct around children (zero tolerance)
  • Treats the gig as side hustle filler — not invested in the brand voice
  • Multi-act generalist style (face paint + balloons + magic + stilts) — dilutes the focus
  • Performative "kids' entertainer" energy — clown character, voice changes, exaggerated personality
  • Active brand-conflict: running their own kids' party brand they aren't willing to dissolve
  • Resistant to brand standards — "I want to do it my way"
  • Can't or won't show up on camera
  • Not in or near the target metro

FAQ block
  1. Do I need to leave my current job? No. Most operators continue running other gigs alongside Kind Bubbles for the first 6 months. The minimum is 30 events / year — average operators ramp to 80–150 events / year by month 12.
  2. Is there a franchise fee? Not in this first wave. The first 5 operators come in on a no-upfront-fee partnership. After the first 5, the structure formalizes with a franchise lawyer.
  3. Who pays for materials and supplies? Operator covers materials at-cost (we send a starter kit). All marketing, content, and lead generation is on us.
  4. What if I'm not in one of the first 5 cities? Apply anyway. We're tracking demand by metro to inform city #6+ rollout.
  5. What does the training week cost? Travel + lodging covered. Operator's only cost is taking the week off.
  6. What if I have my own brand already? We discuss it on the screening call. Most operators come from independent backgrounds — there's a path that protects both sides.

Where to source · per metro

Pre-screened operator pools
by city.

Operator-quality candidates exist in every major metro. The trick is finding them in the right pools — not on Craigslist, not on Indeed, not in big franchise expos. The right candidates are 1–2 degrees from people who already do this work and trust each other.

Best sources
  • Children's museum educators (e.g., Bay Area Discovery Museum, Tech Interactive)
  • Pediatric / sensory-play OT and PT therapists
  • Working actors / improv folks who already do kids' birthday gigs
  • Outschool / KiwiCo-adjacent online educators looking for in-person work
  • Camp counselors or summer-camp directors with off-season capacity
  • Former teachers (especially elementary, Montessori, Waldorf)
  • Doulas and postpartum specialists with established parent trust networks
  • Local entertainers tagged on @kind.bubbles or in the Bay Area Map peer set (D06)
Avoid sourcing from
  • GigSalad / The Bash / Thumbtack (mostly multi-act party generalists)
  • Franchise expos (wrong audience entirely)
  • Indeed / LinkedIn / Craigslist for "kids' entertainer"
  • Anyone running a competing kids' birthday brand they won't let go of
