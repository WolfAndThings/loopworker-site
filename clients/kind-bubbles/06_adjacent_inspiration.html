The Frame

Why adjacent beats direct for format ideas.

Module 02 mapped the ten people you actually compete with for booking dollars. This module does the opposite — it scans seven lanes you don't compete in, looking for one specific borrow each. Not who to fear. Who to study.

The reason this matters: if Chris only watches other bubble vendors, he ends up looking like another bubble vendor. The interesting moves come from ports — a single dad's confessional cadence, a CPG founder's TikTok founder-story format, a calm-parenting carousel structure, a chemistry teacher's reveal beat. Bring those shapes home, fill them with bubbles + biochemist-dad, and you own a hybrid no one else can copy.

"You don't need a new bubble. You need a format your competitors haven't read yet."— The Adjacent Read

Each card maps the borrow back to one of your four pillars: Bubble Science, Founder Story, Event Magic, or Parent Connection.

Lane 01 · Single-Dad Creators

The emotional-vulnerability lane

Chris's deepest moat is the biochemist-to-single-dad arc. These creators already mine that vein at scale — study how they sequence honesty, mundanity, and humor without becoming sob-content.

@theeverythingdad / Dylan Mondo
National · 1M IG · solo-parent POV
What he does

Single dad of four. Day-in-the-life vlogs of the boring stuff — making dinner, doing his daughter's hair, tidying. Not flashy. Not edited. The mundanity is the brand.

What Chris can steal Founder Story

The "morning before a Saturday booking" reel. Pre-event POV — packing the car, mixing solution in the kitchen, making his daughter pancakes before he leaves. Show the dad-life that produced the show. No script.

Isaac Rochell · @isaacrochell
National · 2M+ TikTok · NFL girl-dad
What he does

Former NFL player turned full-time girl-dad creator. Plays into the "tough guy with a daughter" wedge. Format is short, playful POVs of him doing girl-dad tasks badly and then improving on camera.

What Chris can steal Founder Story

The "[unexpected credential] dad does [soft kid task]" frame. For Chris: "Biochemist dad attempts a 4-year-old's tea party". The contrast between hard credential and soft moment is the entire engine.

Zeth & Saylor · @zeth
National · 13M TikTok · dad-daughter duo
What they do

Tattooed dad, tiny daughter, deadpan banter. Format is just the kid talking — Saylor's "magical ice powers" bit, her takes on adult topics. Dad as straight-man, kid as star.

What Chris can steal Parent Connection

Make his daughter the on-camera scientist sometimes. Hand her the wand. Let her explain bubbles in 4-year-old terms. Parents don't engage with experts; they engage with kids who remind them of their own.

Lane 02 · Science-On-Camera Kids Lane

The "wait, that's how that works?" creators

You're a biochemist, not a performer. These two are the proof that science-as-content can scale to millions when the demo is tight, the reveal is fast, and the educator is the brand.

@thedadlab / Sergei Urban
National · 1M IG · 10M total · DIY-science dad
What he does

Dad-runs-experiments-with-his-two-sons format. Every video is a single in-home demo, 15-30s, with a clear visual payoff. Kid is in frame. Dad does the work. No lab coat, no studio.

What Chris can steal Bubble Science

The "one phenomenon, one demo, daughter-in-frame" reel template. Pick a single bubble property — surface tension, refraction, soap film geometry — show it in 20 seconds, with his kid reacting. Weekly cadence. Becomes a content engine.

@chemteacherphil / Phil Cook
National · 3.8M TikTok · classroom chemistry
What he does

Indiana high-school chem teacher. Films from his actual classroom. Setup-tension-reveal in under 30 seconds. Often features his students reacting in real time.

What Chris can steal Bubble Science

The 3-beat structure: (1) "Most people think bubbles are just soap and water" → (2) show the molecule diagram for 2 seconds → (3) the bubble does the unexpected thing on camera. Borrow the structure verbatim.

Lane 03 · Calm-Parenting Lane

The voice-of-trust creators

Module 02 already covered Big Little Feelings + Busy Toddler as aspirational. This goes deeper: three more accounts that own the calm, evidence-backed register Bay Area parents recognize as trustworthy.

@janetlansbury · Janet Lansbury
National · 393K IG · respectful-parenting OG
What she does

Author of No Bad Kids. Hosts the Unruffled podcast (60M+ downloads). Her IG is mostly text-on-color tiles + voice clips — calm, slow, no theatrics. She sounds like the parent the audience wants to be.

What Chris can steal Parent Connection

Quiet voice-over reels. Chris narrating one short calm thought ("birthday parties shouldn't feel chaotic — here's the trick I use") over 8 seconds of slow-mo bubble footage. Tone-shift from "performer" to "guide".

@solidstarts · Jenny Best
National · 4M IG · evidence-based
What they do

Pediatric-pro-led account on baby-led weaning. Refused VC, refused ads. Every post is "here's the science, here's the safe way, here's the food cut correctly." Trust through specificity.

What Chris can steal Parent Connection

The "one safety detail no one mentions" post. For Chris: "Why most bubble solution stings kids' eyes — and what I use instead." Position credibility through what you refuse to do, not what you offer.

@tinyhood · Tinyhood
National · 106K IG · on-demand parenting ed
What they do

Pediatrician + therapist-led parenting courses. Heavy save-bait carousels: "5 things to say when your toddler melts down at a party." Specific, actionable, non-judgemental.

What Chris can steal Event Magic

The "5 things to say" carousel — but for kids' birthdays. "5 things I say when a kid is too shy to come into the bubble circle." Save-bait + utility = parents share it with other parents. Free distribution.

Lane 04 · Magic / Wonder-Reaction Lane

The "kid's mind blown" performers

Magicians are the closest format-cousin to bubble performers — same setup/payoff/reaction structure, same "wonder is the product" thesis. One creator is enough; Justin Willman is the modern blueprint.

@justinwillman · Justin Willman
National · 540K IG · Netflix's Magic For Humans
What he does

Comedy-magician who built a Netflix special on street magic + audience reactions. Every reel is the same shape: setup the trick in 5s, perform in 5s, hold the camera on the spectator's face for the final 5s. The reaction is the post.

What Chris can steal Event Magic

Stop framing yourself as the subject. Frame the kid's face as the subject. Shoot 50% of every gig from behind the bubble looking back at the kid. The reaction shot is the reel — your bubble is just the foreground blur. This is the single biggest visual upgrade available to you.

Lane 05 · Tactile / Sensory-Satisfaction Lane

The slime + slow-mo physics nerds

Bubbles are sensory content already — they just haven't been shot like sensory content. The slime queens and slow-mo physics accounts crack the visual grammar of "satisfying." Borrow the camera, not the topic.

@karinagarc1a · Karina Garcia
National · 1.5M IG · 9M YouTube · OG slime queen
What she does

Built a $1M+/year empire on slime ASMR. Macro shots, no music or just texture sounds, hands always in frame. Camera 6 inches from the slime. Pacing is hypnotic.

What Chris can steal Bubble Science

Macro-ASMR cut of bubble solution being mixed. iPhone 6 inches above the bowl, phone-mic only, no voice-over. Just glycerin pouring, the swirl, the lift. 10-second sensory loops between his louder reels. Clean palate cleansers.

The Slow Mo Guys · @gavinfree
National · 14M+ YouTube · fluid-dynamics gold
What they do

Surface tension droplets at 2,500fps. Spinning-water galaxies. Their thesis: ordinary physics, filmed at impossible frame rates, becomes the most engaging visual on the internet.

What Chris can steal Bubble Science

iPhone slow-mo (240fps) of a bubble popping, paired with a voice-over that lasts the same 1.5s explaining the physics. The pop is the hook. Three frames of explanation is the science. Loop it. This is your highest-shareability format.

Lane 06 · Founder-On-Camera CPG Lane

The single-founder-as-brand archetype

Three founders who built billion-dollar valuations partly by being on camera themselves. Different category from yours, but the format/cadence of "founder explains the why" translates 1:1 to a local-service business — and almost no Bay Area service founders are doing it.

@allisonellsworth · Poppi founder
National · 1M+ cross-platform · sold to PepsiCo $1.95B
What she does

Face of Poppi. Posts kitchen-iteration TikToks ("here's me reformulating the lemon flavor"), Shark Tank flashbacks, pregnant-on-camera energy. Founder = brand. Audience = parasocial.

What Chris can steal Founder Story

The "founder iterating in the kitchen" reel. Chris filming himself testing bubble formulations in the family kitchen, narrating one tweak ("I added 5% more glycerin this morning"). It's the work, not the show. Parents like buying from people who care about their craft.

Mike Cessario · Liquid Death
National · brand 8M+ IG · founder rarely on-camera but voice is everywhere
What he does

Doesn't post selfies — but every Liquid Death piece sounds like Mike. Thesis: brand voice is the founder's voice, even when the founder isn't physically on camera. Pure tone-of-voice consistency.

What Chris can steal Founder Story

Pick three words that are Chris-on-the-page even when Chris isn't on-screen. Suggestion: quiet, curious, careful. Caption everything in that register. The founder voice carries the brand even on event-recap reels where Chris isn't speaking.

Ben Goodwin · Olipop CEO
National · brand 3M+ IG · founder featured on @drinkolipop
What he does

College dropout obsessed with the gut microbiome. Olipop's TikTok regularly features him in 30-60s explainer pieces — "why this ingredient", "what gut bacteria actually do". Nerd founder, calmly explaining. Trust through depth.

What Chris can steal Bubble Science

The "calm nerd explainer" cadence. Sit-down, framed at chest-up, talking into the camera for 45 seconds about one bubble property. No cuts. No jokes. Slow Olipop-Ben energy. Differentiates immediately from every shouty bubble performer.

Lane 08 · Bay Area Parent-Discovery Lane

Hyper-local creators your buyer already follows

The most underrated lane. These aren't national stars — they're Bay Area parents posting Bay Area kid activities for other Bay Area parents. The audience is exactly Kind Bubbles' buyer, geographically pre-sorted. One UGC mention from one of these can fill a weekend's bookings.

Bay Area parent-creator pipeline
Apify-discoverable · 20-account starter sheet
How to build the list

Search Instagram for: "bayarea kids", "sf moms", "marincountymom", "paloaltoparent", "berkeleymom", "bayareafamily", "bayareatoddler", "sfmoms". Most have 5K–80K followers. Most have public emails or DMs. Most are dying for content beyond playground reviews. Filter for Bay Area-located accounts that already post about kid-friendly experiences.

What Chris can do here Parent Connection

Two moves. (1) Send each account an unannounced free Kind Bubbles experience — small private event, no obligation. Highest-conversion outreach format for hyper-local creators is "experience first, ask never." (2) Pitch a "Bubble Science Saturday" co-hosted public class — they promote to their audience, Kind Bubbles donates the experience, both brands grow. Recurring quarterly gives a community ritual.

Phase 2 build

Build a sheet of 20 Bay Area parent-discovery accounts. Send each one a free Kind Bubbles weekend experience over six months. Even 30% conversion = 6 unprompted UGC posts to perfectly-targeted audiences. Cost: 6 free events. Return: probably 2–4 corporate or repeat-private bookings each.

Conclusion · Five highest-leverage borrows

Of the 17 ideas in this module, these five are worth shooting first.

Most of the cards above are nice-to-haves. These five compound — meaning each one builds an asset (a format, a tone, a recurring frame) that pays out across many future reels, not just one. Order them by how easy they are to film with what you already own.

Borrow 01 · From Justin Willman
"The reaction shot, not the bubble, is the post."

Pillar: Event Magic · Film first: Next gig — instruct yourself to spend 50% of recording time pointed at the kid's face with the bubble out-of-focus in the foreground. One reel, that orientation flipped, will outperform every front-facing bubble shot you've ever made.

Borrow 02 · From The Slow Mo Guys + Karina Garcia
"iPhone slo-mo + 1.5s of voice-over = your highest-share format."

Pillar: Bubble Science · Film first: A 240fps pop on the kitchen counter, voice-over: "this is what surface tension looks like when it lets go." Three minutes to shoot. Three months of reach.

Borrow 03 · From @theeverythingdad
"Pre-event POV — the dad behind the show."

Pillar: Founder Story · Film first: Saturday morning before a booking. Mixing solution at the kitchen sink. Daughter at the table. Voiceover: "This is the part nobody books — but it's the part I love." 30 seconds. Becomes your pinned post.

Borrow 04 · From Tinyhood + Solid Starts
"5 things I say when a kid is too shy to come into the bubble circle."

Pillar: Parent Connection · Film first: A 5-slide carousel, text-on-color, no faces. Save-bait. The parent saves it for their next party regardless of who runs it — but next time they're hiring, you're the only name they remember.

Borrow 05 · From Ben Goodwin (Olipop)
"Sit-down, no cuts, 45 seconds, one bubble property."

Pillar: Bubble Science · Film first: Tripod. Chest-up frame. One take. "Why bubbles are always spheres — even when you blow them through a square wand." No edit. No music. Slow nerd energy. Differentiates instantly from every shouty performer in the lane.

Each borrow above maps to a different pillar — meaning together they build a balanced first month of content that covers Bubble Science (×2), Founder Story, Event Magic, and Parent Connection. That's the entire shape of the brand, from five reels.
