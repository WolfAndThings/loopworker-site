How to use these

Each hook is a 4-line kit, not just a sentence.

Every hook below has four parts:

(1) The hook line — the actual first line of the post or the first second of the reel.
(2) On camera — the line you say out loud (voiceover or face-to-camera).
(3) On screen — the text overlay that appears on the video in the first 1–2 seconds.
(4) First shot — what the camera is actually pointed at when the hook lands.

Most reel failures are good lines paired with the wrong opening shot. The kit format below is designed to keep all four locked together so the hook lands in 1.5 seconds the way it's supposed to.

"A good hook is a sentence a parent can't finish in their head. They have to watch the next two seconds to know how it ends."— The Hook Test

3 Worked Examples

Three example reel structures — hook → middle → CTA.

Each example uses a hook from above as its opening, then keeps the promise across the next 25–60 seconds.

Reel 01 · Founder & Family · 60s · Hook #01

"Why did we start Kind Bubbles?"

0–2s · Hook
On-screen text: "Why did we start Kind Bubbles?" Founder face, eye contact.
2–10s · Tension
"What started as a simple idea — bringing bubbles to local events — quickly became something bigger."
10–45s · Open Loop
B-roll of the first event, your daughter helping, parents reacting, the moment you realized this was a real thing.
45–55s · Payoff
"We do this because the world needs more moments of pure joy. That's not a marketing line. That's the actual reason."
55–60s · CTA
"Drop a 🫧 in the comments if you've ever experienced a Kind Bubbles event. Or DM 'birthday' to book your kid's next one."

"Hello to something they'll actually remember. No DJ. No trending music. No grand finale."
8–22s · Payoff
B-roll: kids in a giant bubble cloud, parents putting phones down, the room going quiet.
22–30s · CTA
"Now booking 2026 Bay Area + Sacramento parties. KindBubbles.com or DM 'summer'."
Reel 03 · Bubble Science · 45s · Hook #18

"Try our FREE bubble recipe — let your little scientists experiment."

0–2s · Hook
