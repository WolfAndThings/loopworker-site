Phase 1 · Social Audit + Content
How to use

One hook, one line, one job — buy 1.5 more seconds.

A hook does not have to be clever. It has to be specific. The audit found that on @kind.bubbles, every winning post had a face, a question, or a number in the first 1.5 seconds. Every losing post had a setup shot or a vibe word. So these hooks all pass that test: noun, number, question, or named moment. No "magical". No "amazing". No "vibes".

Each hook is tagged by pillar (Founder & Family, Reaction & Show, Bubble Science, Counter-Position) and by archetype — Question, Identity Stack, Counter-Position, First-Time Frame, Insider Truth, Numeric, Confession.

"A good hook is a sentence a parent can't finish in their head. They have to watch the next two seconds to know how it ends."— The Hook Test

3 Worked Examples

Three example reel structures — hook → middle → CTA.

A hook only works if the next 25 seconds keep the promise. Here are three full reel skeletons, one per pillar, showing how the hooks above carry the rest of the script.

Reel 01 · Founder & Family · 75s

"Single dad. Bubble guy. Now this."

0–2s · Hook
Founder face-to-camera, on-screen text: "Single dad. Bubble guy. Now this." Cut to wand mid-bubble.
2–15s · Tension
"Three years ago I was teaching chemistry. Today I run pop-up bubble parties for kids. Here's how I got from there to here."
15–55s · Open Loop
Voice over B-roll: the hard year, the daughter, the first event in the backyard, the moment a parent cried, the moment he realized this was a business.
55–70s · Payoff
"The thing about being a single dad — you stop asking what's impressive. You only ask what works." Cut to wide of an event: faces.
70–75s · CTA
"If your kid's birthday is coming up — DM me. I want to hear about them before I quote anything."
Reel 02 · Bubble Science · 60s

"Bubbles are technically three layers thick. Most people think one."

0–2s · Hook
On-screen text: "Bubbles are 3 layers." Founder face, holding a single bubble between fingers.
2–8s · Tension
"Most people think a bubble is one layer of soap. It's actually three — soap, water, soap."
8–35s · Open Loop
Cross-section illustration / animation. Founder explains why that matters at a kid's birthday: "the middle layer is what decides whether your kid can touch it without it popping."
35–55s · Payoff
B-roll of a kid touching a giant bubble — and it not popping. "This is what a 3-layer bubble looks like, in a backyard, with a 5-year-old."
55–60s · CTA
"Bay Area · book a Kind Bubbles party · link in bio."
Reel 03 · Counter-Position · 45s

"Why we don't do face paint or balloons. (And what we do instead.)"

0–2s · Hook
Bold on-screen text: "We don't do face paint." Founder face, half-smile, soft fade-in.
2–10s · Tension
"Every kids' party I've ever been to does face paint and balloons. We don't. Three reasons —"
10–35s · Open Loop
Three-beat list, on-screen captions: 1. Most face paint isn't kid-safe. 2. Balloons are an environmental own-goal. 3. Both make the parent the cleanup crew. We don't do that to the family that hired us.
35–42s · Payoff
"What we do instead is make sure your kid is the only thing anyone is looking at." Cut to a face mid-laugh.
42–45s · CTA
"Tag a parent who needs to read this. Or save it for the next party you're planning."
