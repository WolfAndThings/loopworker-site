Why this order

Social → Web → Blog.
Not at the same time.

It's tempting to try and launch all three at once — but that's the move that usually leads to a half-built site, a few half-finished blog posts, and an IG that doesn't get the love it needs. The right order is sequential — each engine sets up the next one to actually work.

Why social first
Founder voice has to land before anything else gets built. The voice on IG and TikTok is the brand voice. Once it's established, every web page, every blog post, every operator pitch, every sales email rides that voice. Build the voice → build the site to fit it.
Why web second
Web turns followers into bookings. Without a strong site, social traffic dies on landing. The site has to be built around what the social audience actually clicks toward — booking, operator-recruitment, founder authority. Voice tells the site what to say.
Why blog third
Blog is the SEO + authority play that pays in 6–12 months. Built last because (1) it needs the founder voice locked, (2) it needs the site architecture to plug into, and (3) Google rewards depth over speed. Slow burn, biggest long-term lever.
The biggest mistake founders make is treating social, web, and blog as separate workstreams. They're one engine, started in three stages. Each one inherits the voice from the one before it. The sequencing principle

The founder voice is the brand voice. The brand voice is the moat. Big Little Feelings (3.5M) and Dr. Becky (3.5M) both built massive followings before launching their courses, books, and products. Tech stack recommendation
Webflow. Best balance of design control, Chris being able to edit content himself, blog CMS, SEO, performance. Avoid: Squarespace (too rigid for the city pages), WordPress (too maintenance-heavy), pure custom code (too slow to iterate). Webflow CMS handles city pages + blog as templated collections.
Design direction
Editorial. Calm. Founder-centric. Bigger photos, fewer columns, more white space. Cormorant Garamond serif headlines + Inter body (already in the deliverables you've seen). Color palette: warm cream, deep ink, single gold accent. Less "kids' party site" — more "national parenting brand" → think Lovevery + The Atlantic.

Engine 03 · weeks 9–24+
03
Blog · SEO · Long-Form Authority
Therapy Dad content compounds for years · 1 post / week · evergreen
Weeks 9–24+
The thesis
SEO is the only marketing channel that compounds. Reels die in 7 days. Blog posts rank for years. The right blog strategy makes Kind Bubbles the answer when a parent in Atlanta searches "how to talk to a shy kid at a birthday party" three years from now — and the operator in Atlanta gets the booking. Slow burn. Biggest long-term lever.
Keyword strategy
Three keyword tiers, ranked by intent.
TierExamplesWhy
High intent"bubble party san francisco" · "kids birthday entertainer marin" · "school assembly bay area"Direct booking. Operator-routable. Lower volume but converts.
Authority intent"how to handle a shy kid at a party" · "sensory play activities for toddlers" · "single dad parenting"Builds Therapy Dad authority. Pulls parent audience. Indirect booking driver.
Adjacent intent"science behind bubbles" · "non-toxic bubble solution recipe" · "best birthday party themes for 6 year olds"Wide net. SEO traffic that converts to email signups, then to bookings.
