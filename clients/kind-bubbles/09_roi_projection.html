What we're really building

The asset is bigger than
the Year 1 cash.

Every number above is direct cash from bookings. The bigger thing we want to build is the asset — what exists at month 13 that didn't exist at month 0. Most of the real value lands in Year 2 and beyond.

What we want Kind Bubbles to own by month 13

  • An audience asset of 8K – 75K engaged Bay Area parents · ranges $40K – $400K in valuation depending on engagement quality. Sellable, leverageable, recurring.
  • A founder personal brand · Chris becomes the named expert in the Bay Area sensory-play / wellness-coded children's experiences niche. Press, podcasts, panels, partnerships.
  • A library of 192 reels + 48 carousels + 192 stories + an evergreen blog · re-runnable, repurposable, recoverable as Year 2 content backbone with zero new shoot cost.
  • A booking pipeline that runs on inbound · stop chasing leads. Inquiries fill the inbox without paid ads. Pipeline value alone >$50K/yr.
  • SEO + Google ranking on Bay Area kids' party queries · Kind Bubbles already ranks #1 for "sensory play bubble bay area" (D07 finding). Phase 2 cements this and expands to 10–30 queries.
  • Corporate gifting and B2B contract foundations · once Cisco, Genentech, or a tech company books once, they often book annually. Corporate accounts are the highest LTV in this category.
  • Optionality on adjacent products · with audience + brand, the door opens for: bubble juice line, sensory-play toy line, online course for parents, book deal, retreat program. Each one is its own revenue stream Year 2+.

Honest assumptions

What we'd need to be true —
and what would break it.

Where the numbers come from

Pricing benchmarks: Verbatim from D07 (Bay Area Competitor Map, Apify-scraped May 1). SF Bubble Man's $450 standard rate, The Bubble Lady's $390–$500 published rate card. Kind Bubbles' premium tier $475–$650 sits just above the market.

Audience growth ranges: Compared to Apify-verified Bay Area peers — @bayarea_toddlerexplorer at 37K, bubble category creators @atlantabubbleshow ~12K, @kieronthemighty 1.2M likes on a single viral. Conservative = no viral. Realistic = 2–3 modest hits. Aggressive = 1–2 truly viral.

Conversion rates: 0.7%–1.5% follower-to-booking annual conversion — typical for high-intent local service businesses with strong founder narrative. Lower bound used in conservative case.

Capacity ceiling: Chris is one person. Solo capacity caps at ~150 events/year (≈3 per week). Aggressive scenario assumes a 2nd-performer hire mid-Year 1.

What would break the model

Inconsistent posting — under 8 reels/month consistently drops growth to flat. The cadence in D08 is the floor, not the ceiling.

No founder-on-camera content — Chris's personal story (biochemist + single-dad + healing) is the unfair advantage. If founder content stays under 30% of feed, growth caps.

No B2B outreach — D07 corporate target list (Google, Cisco, Genentech, etc.) needs to be worked. Inbound only ≠ corporate revenue.

Geographic dilution — chasing national reach early dilutes the Bay Area conversion model. Stay local first 12 months.

What we can't promise

Specific numbers in any of the three scenarios are speculative. Content marketing depends on luck (one viral hit changes the math), execution quality (founder showing up on camera or not), market timing (a competitor launching big the same month would slow growth), and dozens of other factors. What this is: our shared target, anchored to real Apify data, that says "a range like this is what we want to be aiming at."
