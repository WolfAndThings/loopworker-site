01 · The Read

The feed is shipping event recaps in a market that books on parental relief.

Across 55 posts in 21 months, the dominant pattern is recap content — bubbles in the air, kids in motion, captions that describe what's already on screen. It's pretty. It performs at a baseline (median 12 likes, median 147 video views). But almost none of it gives a scrolling parent a reason to stop.

The two posts that broke through the baseline did one of two things: they showed a face (the 370-like still from Sept 2024 — a kid mid-reaction), or they made a founder claim ("Why did we start Kind Bubbles?" — 55 likes, 1,046 views, the highest-watched founder post in the feed).

The fix isn't more posts. It's pulling more of the feed toward the two patterns that already work — and cutting the recap volume that the algorithm has learned to ignore.

"Your two best posts in 21 months were a kid's face and a founder claim. Everything else is variations on a recap. The leverage is to stop hiding the two things that already work."— The TL;DR

05 · Format Mix — Reality vs Target

The current ratio.

Across the 55 posts: 45 Reels (81%) · 7 Carousels (13%) · 3 Stills (5%). Reel-dominant is correct for 2026. The actual gap is too few carousels — and almost no parent-utility carousels.

Reels Now45 of 55 · 81% Target~70%
Carousels Now7 of 55 · 13% Target~25%
Stills Now3 of 55 · 5% Target~5%

The shift: keep stills rare (the one captionless still at 370 likes shows they work when used sparingly), and roughly double the carousel volume — specifically save-bait carousels (rainy-day sensory tips, screen-free birthday checklists, what's-in-our-event-kit). Carousels are the format Instagram rewards with saves, and saves push the whole account up.

07 · Hook Patterns Across 55

The opening line of every post.

Sorted by how the first six words of the caption read to a scrolling parent.

In Heavy Use · The Statement Open

30 of 55. Most common pattern — caption begins with a flat description of the post ("We had a blast…", "Another hour on the iPad…", "What a great time…"). Performance is mixed — these are filler unless the first sentence does the work.

In Use · The Question Open

8 of 55. Includes your highest-watched post in the entire feed ("Why did we start Kind Bubbles?" — 1,046 views). Underused.

In Use · The Direct CTA Open

5 of 55. "DM us for the ticket link", "Comment 'BUBBLE' for the registration". Two of these are in the top 10. Pattern works when paired with a date-bound offer.

No Caption / Cold Open

5 of 55. Includes your #1 post by likes (370). Empty captions work when the visual carries. Don't overuse — but don't avoid.

Rare · The First-Person Story Open

3 of 55. "It started with a simple idea…" Underused for an account whose founder story is its strongest asset. Should be 1 of every 6 posts, currently 1 of every 18.

Missing · The Counter-Position Open

1 of 55. "Say goodbye to bounce houses…" → 1,422 views, top 10. The most efficient way to get a tag/comment, and the feed has used it once.

Missing · The Numbered List Open

0 of 55. "5 ways…", "3 things…", "The 7-step…". This is what carousels are made for. Combined with the missing Parent Connection pillar — this is the largest single hole.

"In 21 months: one counter-position post, zero numbered-list posts, three first-person founder posts. The three highest-leverage hook patterns are also the three least-used."— The Open-Line Diagnostic
08 · Save / Share / DM Signals

What the algorithm reads as parental trust.

Reach is decided by likes. Saves and shares decide whether a post is reshown. DMs decide whether the algorithm re-prioritizes the entire account. The breakdown across 55 posts:

CTA Mix · Measured

10 of 55 posts say "DM us" · 3 use a comment-trigger ("Comment 'bubble'") · 3 use "link in bio" · 28 of 55 have no CTA at all. Half the feed leaves the bookable moment on the table.

Comments · Where They Cluster

71 comments on the May 31 event launch. 52 comments on the Early Bird post. The next-highest is 9. Two posts hold 70% of all caption-driven comments in the entire feed. Both used the same comment-trigger formula.

Collab Tags · The Multiplier

The two posts above 200 likes both tagged a partner account (@fabricated.fantasy at #2; @bayarea_toddlersplay at #9). Cross-account distribution is the single fastest reach lever and is currently used in roughly 1 of every 5 posts.

Saves · The Empty Lane

Save-bait content (parent-utility carousels) = 0 in 55 posts. Saves are the single strongest signal IG uses to surface a post to similar audiences. This is a free lane.

09 · The Five Highest-Leverage Moves

If you do nothing else for 30 days, do these five.

  1. Open the Parent Connection lane. Zero posts in 21 months. Ship one save-bait carousel every 5 posts (rainy-day sensory ideas, screen-free birthday checklist, what's-in-our-bubble-kit). This is the single largest unbuilt opportunity on the account.
  2. Repeat the comment-trigger formula on every event. Two of your top six posts used "Comment '[word]' for the link". The data is unambiguous — this works on this account specifically. Make it the default for every date-bound post.
  3. Triple the founder-story output. Currently 1 founder-origin post per year. Your highest-watched post (1,046 views) is in this lane. Run one founder reel per month minimum — face-to-camera, 60 seconds, no music.
  4. Add a CTA to every post. 28 of 55 have none. Even a soft one ("DM us if you're planning a 2026 birthday") doubles the bookable surface area without adding a single new post.
  5. Plan one collab tag per month. Both 200+ like posts had a partner tag. Schedule them in advance — don't wait for one to happen.
