Event Capture & Tag System

Every show,
same capture.
Every post,
same tags.

A repeatable system Chris runs at every Kind Bubbles event — what to shoot, what to capture, what to tag, what to post. Built so a show in Walnut Creek and a show in Sacramento and a show in San Jose all produce content that ladders into the same four pillars (D03), uses the same call-outs, and gets the same algorithmic lift.

For: Chris Steele · used at every event going forward
What it covers: Pre-show prep · 12 mandatory shots · 5 reel clips · the tag stack · call-out templates · permission flow
Pairs with: D03 (pillars) · D04 (hooks) · D07 (sprint) — this is the operational layer underneath all three
Built so every show generates content. Not just the special ones.
Why a system

The audit found 21 of 55 posts are filler.

The single biggest content leak on @kind.bubbles is the gap between events you ran and posts that came from them. Some events generate ten posts; others generate zero. The result: a feed that ships when there's time and goes silent when there isn't.

The fix is operational, not creative. One capture sheet. One tag stack. One call-out library. Every show. When the system is the same every time, the post-show content takes 20 minutes — not three hours of "what should we even post about this one."

"You've already done the hard work — you ran the show. The capture system makes sure that work compounds into content, every time, without thinking."— The Job
The flow

Five stages. Same every show.

Each stage has a small, fixed checklist. Total time investment: ~10 minutes pre-show, 5 minutes during, 20 minutes post. That's it.

01
Pre-show prep
10 min · night before or morning of
  • Geo-pin set on phone. Drop the location pin in your camera roll so every photo + video tags the venue automatically.
  • Tripod + iPhone packed. Plus charger. Plus the lav mic if it's a founder-voice day.
  • Permission slips printed (or QR code to digital form) — see the permission section below.
  • Partner accounts noted. Who's at the show? Co-host, venue, parent creator, photographer? Get the @ handle before you're on site.
  • Post intent picked. Which pillar is the post going to be? Founder & Family · Reaction & Show · Bubble Science · Counter-Position. Picking before the show changes what you shoot.
02
During the show — capture
5 min · across the event
  • The 12 mandatory shots (full list in next section). Get them all. Treat them like a ritual — same shots every show, even if they look the same as last week's. The library compounds.
  • 4 video clips — 5 to 10 seconds each — covering the four content pillars: a kid's face mid-reaction, a science moment (giant bubble forming, slow-mo pop), a founder moment (you handing a wand, you talking to a kid), a counter-position moment (a parent putting their phone down, a kid choosing this over a tablet).
  • One audio clip — kids cheering, the room going quiet, that "oh!" moment when the bubble doesn't pop. Audio is half of a reel.
  • Permission gathered for any kid whose face you'll show in a post (see Stage 5).
03
Within 1 hour after — quick story
5 min · while still on site or in the car
  • Post one Story. A 5-second clip. Geo-tag the location. Tag any partner @. "That was Mira's 6th birthday. Bubbles Bay Area-style." Done. Don't overthink it.
  • Save the best 3 clips to a "post-ready" folder on your phone. These are the ones the next-day reel gets cut from.
  • DM the host the link to your booking page with a "thank you for letting us share the day" line. Builds re-booking + referrals.
04
Within 24 hours — the post
20 min · the next day
  • Pick the pillar (the one you set in Stage 1) and the matching hook from D04.
  • Cut the reel or carousel using the 12-shot library. Most reels are 15–30 seconds; carousels are 4–6 slides.
  • Apply the tag stack (full template in the next section): location · event type · pillar · partner @ · Bay Area discovery hashtags.
  • Apply the relevant call-out (templates below): client thank-you, venue shoutout, partner credit, geographic SEO line.
  • Schedule, don't post live. Schedule for the right time slot per pillar (D03 sequencing).
05
Within 1 week — the second post
15 min · 4–7 days after the event
  • Most events should generate 2 posts, not 1. The day-after post is the reel; the week-after post is a different angle from the same event — usually a Counter-Position carousel ("Three things this birthday party didn't have, and why parents thanked us") or a Bubble Science save-bait list.
  • Different pillar than the first post. Don't double-up. If the day-after was Reaction & Show, the week-after is Counter-Position or Bubble Science.
  • One event = at least 2 grid posts + 1 Story = 3 pieces of content. Run 4 events / month and you have 12 posts before any other source kicks in.
The capture sheet

Twelve shots. Every event. No exceptions.

Borrowed from the wedding-photographer playbook — a fixed shot list that runs every time. The reason it works: you stop thinking about what to shoot and start thinking about better versions of the shots you already plan to take. Library compounds across every event.

Shot 01 · Setup
The bucket + wands
Lined up before guests arrive. Quiet. Anticipation.
Shot 02 · The first kid
First kid to spot the bubbles
Wide-eye reaction. Often the strongest single image of the day.
Shot 03 · The crowd
All kids running through the cloud
Wide shot. Energy. Use for carousels, not feed.
Shot 04 · Hero bubble
One giant bubble, mid-air, perfectly lit
The "Bubble Science" hero asset. Build a library.
Shot 05 · Hands reaching
Multiple kids' hands reaching up
Detail shot. Reaction & Show pillar.
Shot 06 · The parent laughing
An adult laughing at their kid's reaction
The "I want my kid to be that kid" image. Booking magnet.
Shot 07 · The shy kid
The quietest kid, finally smiling
The most emotional shot. Often becomes a Founder & Family caption.
Shot 08 · Founder + birthday kid
You giving a giant wand to the host child
Founder & Family pillar. Always get one.
Shot 09 · Cake / table
The party setup, bubbles drifting through
Brand-context shot. For showcase + booking page.
Shot 10 · Detail
Close-up of soap film stretching on the wand
Bubble Science pillar. ASMR-friendly.
Shot 11 · Group photo
Kids + you, all smiling
For the host + for "thank you for having us" Story content.
Shot 12 · Strike + thank-you
Empty backyard, gear packed, wave goodbye
"That's a wrap" Story. Quietly emotional.

Plus 4 video clips per event

One clip per pillar — 5 to 10 seconds each.

  • Clip A · Reaction & Show — kid face mid-laugh or mid-wonder, bubble in foreground
  • Clip B · Bubble Science — slow-mo bubble forming, popping, or floating
  • Clip C · Founder & Family — you handing a wand, talking to a kid, watching from the side
  • Clip D · Counter-Position — a parent putting down their phone, a kid ignoring a tablet, the moment everyone goes quiet
The tag stack

Five layers. Every post.

Every post about an event uses the same five-layer tag stack. Builds Bay Area SEO over time and trains the algorithm to put Kind Bubbles in front of the right local audiences.

Layer 01
Location — three layers deep: neighborhood + city + region.
#walnutcreek #eastbay #bayareakids
Why three: Instagram weights specificity. A parent in Walnut Creek searches "walnut creek kids party." A parent in Oakland searches "east bay birthday." Cover both.
Layer 02
Event type — pick one.
#kidsbirthday · #schoolassembly · #corporatewellness · #popupevent · #sensoryday
Don't combine more than one. The algorithm reads "kidsbirthday + corporatewellness" as confused targeting.
Layer 03
Pillar match — tags that signal the pillar to the algorithm.
Founder & Family → #girldad #daddydaughter #familybusiness
Reaction & Show → #kidsreaction #kidssmile #magicofchildhood
Bubble Science → #sensoryplay #stemforkids #asmr
Counter-Position → #screenfree #mindfulparenting #screenfreefun
Layer 04
Partner @ — the multiplier (see D03).
Tag the host, the venue, the photographer, the co-vendor, the parent creator. Every collab tag is a distribution lever.
@bayarea_toddlersplay @kara_styles_parties @walnutcreekrec
Always tag in BOTH the caption AND the post mentions — they surface differently.
Layer 05
Brand discovery — Bay Area parent-discovery hashtags.
#bayareaparents #sfkids #sfmoms #bayareamoms #kindbubbles
Always include #kindbubbles. Builds your branded hashtag library — every post on it is shoppable for repeat visitors.

Total per post: ~12 hashtags. Instagram allows 30, but research shows 8–15 outperforms more. Don't fill the cap — pick the right twelve.

The call-out library

Six paste-ready shoutouts.

Drop into any caption. Each one does a specific job — saying thank you, building geographic SEO, crediting a partner, signaling brand range, framing the booking ask. Use 1–2 per post (not all six).

Call-out 01 · Client thank-you
For the host that booked you
"Thank you to the [BIRTHDAY KID NAME] family in [NEIGHBORHOOD] for letting us bring the bubbles. [MEMORABLE MOMENT FROM THE DAY] is the kind of thing we run this for."
When to use: Day-after post. Always with parent's permission. Replaces "what a magical day" filler.
Call-out 02 · Venue shoutout
For the location
"[VENUE NAME] in [CITY] — one of the best Bay Area backyards/parks/halls we've ever set up in. Tag your friends if you've thrown a party there."
When to use: Public venues only (parks, community centers). Builds SEO + invites tag-prompts.
Call-out 03 · Partner credit
For collaborators on the day
"With @[PARTNER HANDLE] on [STYLING / PHOTOGRAPHY / CO-HOSTING]. Bay Area kids' party people who do it right. Follow them."
When to use: Any event with a co-vendor. Direct algorithmic boost — see D03 multiplier #1 (avg 100♥ on tagged posts vs 28 average).
Call-out 04 · Geographic SEO line
For local search ranking
"Kind Bubbles · sensory bubble parties for [NEIGHBORHOOD], [CITY], and the [REGION] Bay Area · now booking [SEASON] [YEAR]."
When to use: Once per week minimum. Add at the end of the caption. Trains Instagram + Google to rank you for local searches.
Call-out 05 · Booking ask (soft)
The booking line that doesn't sell
"If you're a [BAY AREA / SACRAMENTO] parent with a kid turning [AGE], DM me 'birthday' — I'd love to hear about your kid before I quote anything."
When to use: Post-event reels in the Founder & Family or Reaction & Show pillar. Pairs with a comment-trigger when the post is launching a public event.
Call-out 06 · Range signal
For showing brand depth
"Kind Bubbles also runs school assemblies, corporate wellness pop-ups, and community festivals. Same show, different stage. [SHORT DETAIL ABOUT WHAT HAPPENED THIS WEEK]"
When to use: 1× per month. Reminds the audience the brand has more than birthday parties — opens corporate / school inbound.

Each set is ~12 tags, balanced across the five-layer stack above. Save these in a phone note. Don't write hashtags from scratch every post — pick the matching set, paste, customize layer 04 (the @ partner tag).

The permission flow

Get the sign-off before the bubbles.

The 30-second script · use at every booked event

Most parents say yes — they want their kid in the post. But you need the explicit ask, in writing, before any face-visible content goes up. Run this at booking confirmation, not on the day.

"Hey [PARENT NAME] — quick one. We sometimes share photos and short videos from our events on Instagram and our website. Faces show up sometimes, sometimes they don't — totally up to you. Three options:
(a) Yes, you can show [KID NAME]'s face and tag us;
(b) Yes, but blur or angle around the face;
(c) Please don't post anything from this event.

Whichever you pick, no problem. Just hit reply."

Save the response. A simple Airtable / Notion table with: client name · event date · permission level (a/b/c) · @ handle if they want to be tagged. Now you have a permission ledger you can check before every post.

Note: at public ticketed events, include the photo-release line on the registration form. At school assemblies, the school handles permissions — get a written confirmation from the principal/PTA before posting.

The five highest-leverage moves

If you adopt only five things from this doc, do these.

  1. Run the 12-shot capture sheet at every event. Build the library. Don't wait for the "special" event.
  2. Pick the pillar before the show. 60 seconds of pre-decision changes what you shoot, and the post writes itself the next day.
  3. Apply the five-layer tag stack on every post. Save the five hashtag sets in a phone note. Stop re-writing tags from scratch.
  4. Use a partner @ on at least 1 of every 4 posts. Multiplier #1 from D03 — the data showed avg 100♥ on collab-tagged posts vs ~25 elsewhere. This is free distribution.
  5. Run the permission script at booking, not at the show. Saves the awkward day-of ask. Doubles your usable footage rate.
