How these pillars were chosen

Derived from your feed, not invented.

Every post in the 55-post sample was tagged by what it actually is — origin/family content, reaction footage, science/recipe, counter-position takes — and then performance was measured per tag. The four pillars below are the four clusters that performed and that you already produce. Generic "showcase" posts (n=11, avg 10 likes) got their own bucket — labeled below as the lane to cut.

The two multipliers at the end of this doc — Collab Tag and Comment Trigger — aren't pillars themselves. They're tactics that ride on top of any pillar to multiply reach. Both are measured top performers in the data.

Why these four · what each one does

Each pillar does one specific job.

Each of the four pillars below pulls a different lever:

Founder & Family drives views — origin and dad-daughter content reaches the most people per post.
Reaction & Show drives likes — kid-face footage is the most likeable content on the feed.
Bubble Science drives saves — recipe + technique posts are the lane parents come back to.
Counter-Position drives shares — "what we don't do" content has the highest median views per post.

"A feed running only one of these is a single-instrument band. A feed running all four — with the two multipliers laid on top — is a full record."— The Pillar Logic
Pillar 01 · The Moat

Founder & Family

Job · Drive views. Build trust. The lane parents actually book on.
From the data

16 of 55 posts in the sample fit this pillar (origin, single-dad, daddy-daughter, family business). The highest-watched founder-origin post in the feed — "Why did we start Kind Bubbles?" — sits in this lane at 1,046 video views (7× the median). (Two other posts pulled higher views — 1,880 and 1,422 — but those sit in Reaction & Show and Counter-Position respectively. This is the highest-watched founder post.) The Sacramento Daddy–Daughter Pop-Up carousels (#5 + #8 in the top 10 by likes) are also in this pillar.

Definition

You, on camera, telling the truth. The biochemist-turned-teacher-turned-single-dad arc. The first event for your daughter. The hard year. The why-we-started story. The Daddy–Daughter event format. This pillar is not about bubbles — it's about the man holding the wand and the kid he started this for.

5 example post topics
  • The afternoon I realized this was a business (face-to-camera reel, 60–90s)
  • What being a single dad taught me about how kids actually play
  • My daughter's first Kind Bubbles event — and what it taught me
  • The next Daddy–Daughter Pop-Up (recurring event format — repeat quarterly)
  • One year of Kind Bubbles — what changed, what didn't
3 example hooks
  • Single dad. Bubble guy. Now this.
  • I almost didn't start Kind Bubbles. Here's what changed my mind.
  • The first event I ever did was for my own daughter. I cried in the car after.
Cadence
3–4 of 14 · ~25%
Best format
60–90s reels · face-to-camera
Pillar 02

Reaction & Show

Job · Drive likes. Visual proof — the actual work + the human reaction to it.
From the data

Top performers in this pillar split between kid faces and the show itself — sometimes one, sometimes both in the same frame. Your single highest-engagement post (370 likes, captionless still) is a kid mid-reaction inside a giant bubble — face + craft together. Your highest-views post (1,880 views, "Can your favorite bounce house do THIS?") is a kid disappearing inside a bubble — the trick is the hook. Your "Smoke Bubble Catch" reel pulled 309 views on the trick alone. Both halves of this pillar earn engagement.

Definition

This pillar covers two things, often together:

(1) The Show — your actual work. Giant bubbles forming. Smoke bubbles. Fire + bubble tricks. Bubbles inside bubbles. The trick that took weeks of practice to land. ASMR slow-mo of a wand pulling through solution. The craft on its own — proof that you do this at a level nobody else does.

(2) The Reaction — kid faces, mid-laugh. Parents putting phones down. The 4-year-old's mouth open. The 7-year-old too cool dropping their guard. The room going quiet. The "I want my kid to be that kid" moment.

When both show up in one frame — a kid mid-reaction inside a giant bubble — engagement compounds. That's your 370-like still. Lead with either half. Avoid setup shots and wide pans.

5 example post topics — split across The Show + The Reaction
  • Show: ASMR macro of a giant bubble forming on the wand (no music, sound on, satisfying)
  • Show: Named trick demo — Smoke Bubble Catch · Tunnel of Bubbles · fire + bubbles · bubble-inside-a-bubble (slow-mo, 240fps)
  • Show + Reaction: Kid disappearing inside a giant bubble — optical-illusion trick + wide eyes (your 1,880-view post structure)
  • Reaction: Captionless reaction-shot still — kid face inside a bubble (your 370-like post structure)
  • Reaction: "Watch the older kid stop pretending to be cool" — slow-mo of a 7–10-year-old dropping their guard
3 example hooks · 2 reaction, 1 show
  • This is what 4 years old looks like. (reaction)
  • Watch the older kid stop pretending to be cool. (reaction)
  • The smoke bubble catch — watch what happens when intentional play meets perfect timing. (show)
Cadence
4 of 14 · ~28%
Best format
15–30s reels · face-led OR craft-led · 240fps slow-mo for tricks
Pillar 03

Bubble Science

Job · Drive saves. Build authority. Prove you know more than the next vendor.
From the data

16 of 55 posts already fit this pillar (recipes, surface tension, ASMR, sensory mechanics, freeze-a-bubble). Performance is consistent (~28 likes / 335 video views avg) — the best video-views average across all four pillars. Where this pillar leaks is short captions on teaching content (e.g., "Big bubble recipe" → 2 likes). Captions need to do the teaching the video implies.

Definition

The "how does this actually work" pillar. You're a biochemist — this is the lane where you teach without lecturing. Recipe, surface tension, prismatic light, freeze-a-bubble, why it doesn't sting eyes. The post answers a question the kid is about to ask, and gives the parent a reason to bookmark.

5 example post topics
  • The 3-ingredient recipe I use at every event (numbered carousel — save-bait)
  • Why bubbles always come out round (surface tension, 30s reel)
  • The "three layers" of every bubble (illustrated cross-section)
  • Freeze-a-bubble experiment (winter reel, slow-mo)
  • Why our bubble solution doesn't sting kids' eyes (proof + chemistry)
3 example hooks
  • Bubbles are technically three layers thick. Most people think one.
  • Why does a bubble pop the second your kid touches it? (Spoiler: it's not their fault.)
  • The actual chemistry that makes a giant bubble hold its shape.
Cadence
3 of 14 · ~22%
Best format

Pillar 04 · The under-used lane

Counter-Position

Job · Drive shares + median views. Separate the brand from the category.
From the data

Only 4 of 55 posts use this pattern — and yet it has the highest median views per post in the entire feed (272 vs 147 across other pillars). Your "Goodbye to bounce houses" post pulled 1,422 views — the second-highest video in the sample. This is the most under-built lane on the account.

Definition

"What we don't do, and why." No bounce houses. No balloons. No face paint. No screens. No DJ. No grand-finale fireworks. Each one is a stake in the ground that defines the brand by contrast — and gives the parent a reason to choose you over the generic alternative they were already considering.

5 example post topics
  • "Goodbye to the bounce house. Hello to the bubble that doesn't pop." (proven · 1,422 views)
  • "Why we don't do balloons" (environmental + safety case, carousel)
  • "Why we don't do face paint" (cleanup-on-the-parent argument)
  • "Not your average kids' party" — what makes a Kind Bubbles event different in 30 seconds
  • "Pinterest birthday vs Kind Bubbles birthday" — counter-positioning carousel
3 example hooks
  • Goodbye to the bounce house. Hello to the bubble that doesn't pop.
  • Three things we don't do at a Kind Bubbles party. (And why parents thank us for it.)
  • Most kids' parties make the parent the cleanup crew. Ours doesn't.
Cadence
3 of 14 · ~22%
Best format
30–60s reels · carousels (counter-position lists)
The lane to cut

The "showcase" lane is filler.

11 of 55 posts in your feed don't fit any of the four pillars above. They're generic "look at this bubble" posts, vendor thank-yous, or hashtag-only filler. Average performance: 10 likes — the lowest across all categories. Examples from your feed: "Biz partner" (1♥), "Thanks @oogubievents for a great night in the city!" (3♥), hashtag-only posts (1–2♥).

These aren't bad content — they're private content posted in a public format. They belong in Stories, not the grid. Cutting these and reallocating into the four pillars above is roughly 20% of the feed re-pointed at lanes that already perform.

The two multipliers

Two tactics that ride on top of any pillar.

These aren't pillars — they're mechanics that multiply reach when applied to a post in any pillar. Both are measured top performers in your data.

Multiplier 01 · The Distribution Lever

Collab Tag

Job · Multiply distribution. Borrow another account's audience.
From the data

Posts that tag a partner account average 100 likes — roughly 3× the next-best lane in your feed (n=9). The 280-like Magical Birthday post (#2 in the top 10) carried by @fabricated.fantasy. The 54-like Rose Garden post carried by @bayarea_toddlersplay. Cross-account tagging is the single fastest reach lever you have.

How to use it

Plan one collab per month minimum. Doesn't matter which pillar the post sits in — Founder, Reaction, Science, or Counter-Position. The collab tag is what gets the post seen by the partner's audience too. Pre-schedule them — don't wait for one to "happen."

  • Best partners: Bay Area parent creators, kids' photographers, party stylists, sensory-play educators
  • Pitch trade-experience offers, not paid posts (lower barrier to yes)
  • Always tag with the @ in the caption AND in the post mentions — both surface differently
Multiplier 02 · The Comment Lever

Comment Trigger

Job · Multiply comments. Train the algorithm to surface the post.
From the data

Two posts in your top 10 use this pattern: the May 31 launch (71 comments) and the Early Bird post (52 comments). Together they hold ~70% of all caption-driven comments in the entire 55-post feed. Both used a literal "Comment 'BUBBLE'" prompt. The next-highest comment count on any other post in the feed is 9.

How to use it

Pair every date-bound or offer-bound post (Launch CTAs) with a memorable single-word trigger. The trigger word should be relevant to the offer — "BUBBLE" for an event, "DAD" for a daddy-daughter pop-up, "SUMMER" for booking-window content.

  • One trigger word per post — make it short and uppercase in the caption
  • Reply with the link via DM (preserves the comment count and starts a 1:1 conversation)
  • Use 1× per launch — overuse trains the audience to ignore the prompt
The sequencing

How to order the pillars across a 14-post month.

You don't drop all your Founder & Family content in one week. You don't run three Reaction & Show posts back-to-back. The order is part of the strategy.

Week 1 → Founder & Family · Reaction & Show · Bubble Science · Counter-Position
Week 2 → Reaction & Show · Founder & Family · Bubble Science
Week 3 → Counter-Position · Reaction & Show · Founder & Family · Bubble Science
Week 4 → Founder & Family · Reaction & Show · Counter-Position

Layer on top: One Collab Tag in any of the 14 posts. One Comment Trigger paired with each Launch / event-bound post.

Founder & Family opens the month — sets the trust baseline. Reaction & Show anchors weekends — when parents are scrolling for what to do next Saturday. Bubble Science holds mid-week curiosity slots. Counter-Position lands on Thursday/Sunday — the parent-mental-load timeslots when "wait, why don't they do bounce houses?" lands hardest.

"Every week of every month must contain at least one Founder & Family post and at least one Counter-Position post. The first is your moat. The second is the most under-built lane on your feed."— The Pillar Rule
